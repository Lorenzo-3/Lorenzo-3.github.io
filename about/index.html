About

I’m Lorenzo, a Master’s student in Computer Science at Sapienza University (Rome). I like building things that are practical and measurable: small ML systems, experiments, and pipelines where you can tell what improved and why. What I enjoy most is the moment where messy data starts to make sense, when patterns show up and a real story emerges just from looking at numbers.

More than “computer science for its own sake”, I’m especially drawn to using computation as a tool for statistical study: bioinformatics, physics, game theory, information theory, and any domain where models and data can answer real questions. That said, I still genuinely like computer stuff in general, from code and good engineering to the hands-on side of building and testing systems.

How I work

  • Clarity first: I like simple baselines and clean evaluation.
  • Iterate fast: small experiments, then scale what works.
  • Efficiency matters: I enjoy constraints (edge, speed, memory).

What I build

  • ML pipelines: training + evaluation + interpretation, end to end.
  • Vision experiments: anomaly detection/localization on high-resolution imagery.
  • Graph ML: biological networks, interactomes, and network-medicine ideas.
  • Data analysis: cleaning messy datasets, extracting patterns, telling the story.
